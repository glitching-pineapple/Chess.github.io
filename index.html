Supporting our community through chess

Open Square is a community-centered initiative using chess as a tool for learning, mentorship, confidence, and connection. Through free tutoring, school partnerships, student leadership, and outreach programming, we aim to create meaningful opportunities for young people to grow both on and off the board. Our work is rooted in the belief that chess can strengthen critical thinking, build resilience, and bring people together across schools and neighborhoods.

Mission

Using chess to build confidence, discipline, and opportunity.

Open Square believes chess can open doors for students by developing focus, patience, problem-solving, and self-confidence. We work to make those benefits more accessible through free programming, strong mentorship, and partnerships with schools and community spaces.

What we believe

Every student deserves access to meaningful enrichment and supportive learning environments. We focus on making chess more available in communities where extracurricular opportunities may be limited, while keeping our approach welcoming, inclusive, and community-centered.

How we work

Our programs are designed to be flexible and responsive to community needs. Whether a school wants help launching a chess club, a youth program needs volunteers, or students are looking for free tutoring, Open Square aims to provide thoughtful support that is practical, sustainable, and locally grounded.

We see chess not only as a game, but as a platform for academic growth, mentorship, and stronger community ties.

Programs

Programs designed to grow skills, leadership, and community.

Open Square offers accessible chess programming for students at different stages of learning, from first-time players to those seeking deeper strategy and tournament preparation.

01

Free Chess Tutoring

One-on-one and small-group instruction that helps students build confidence, understand core strategy, and improve through consistent support.

02

School Chess Clubs

Support for starting and sustaining school-based chess communities where students can learn, practice, and grow together.

03

Community Workshops

Introductory and themed workshops for schools, libraries, and youth spaces that make chess welcoming and accessible.

04

Tournament Mentorship

Guidance for students interested in competitive play, including preparation, strategy development, and structured encouragement.
